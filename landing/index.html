Your chats evaporate.
This sticks.

A floating pane plus an MCP server. The things you decide in a chat — call the dentist, email Sarah, schedule the review — don't vanish when the tab closes. They land on the side of your screen and wait until you tick them off.

Get on GitHub How it works

Free. Open source. MIT. BYO key. Works with Claude, OpenRouter, OpenAI, or any OpenAI-compatible endpoint.

Three small moves.

  1. 01

    Claude writes it.

    In any Claude Code or Claude Desktop session, say "I need to call the dentist Friday." Claude calls StickyInc's MCP. A new checkbox appears in your pane before you finish reading its reply.

  2. 02

    You tick it.

    Hover the strip on the right edge of your screen. The pane slides out. Click to complete — the task strikes through, then drops into a quiet archive for tomorrow-you. The line Claude saw next turn says "user completed: call the dentist."

  3. 03

    Calendar gets it.

    Dated items forward to Google Calendar through Claude's own connector. StickyInc doesn't run its own OAuth flow — one less thing to set up, one less place your tokens live.

Why it exists.

Every other AI overlay is summon-ask-dismiss. You open a floating window, ask a thing, close it, and within an hour the answer has evaporated from your brain and the tab.

StickyInc is the inverse. The pane doesn't disappear. It accumulates. The chat is cheap; the commitment graph you build from it over months is what's actually valuable. That graph lives on your machine, in a SQLite file, under your control — not on anyone's server.

Swap Claude for GPT tomorrow. Your pane is still there.
